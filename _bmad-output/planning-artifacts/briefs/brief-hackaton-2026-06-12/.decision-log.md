# Decision Log

## 2026-06-12

- Target Canton Foundation as the primary hackathon prize.
- Treat Privy as optional and useful only if it strengthens the core Canton demo without distracting from Daml/privacy requirements.
- Scope the MVP to physical custody traceability only.
- Attach transportation sheets, invoices, receipts, or similar documents as proof on wallet-to-wallet custody transfers.
- Model supply-chain operational nodes as wallets/ledger parties: production sites, transports, storages, and port storages.
- Preserve company-level privacy: companies should not see other companies' products, balances, storage locations, or transactions unless explicitly involved or authorized.
- Keep the product commodity-generic with a selector; use certified coffee beans and cacao as seeded demo examples.
- Optimize the MVP attestation around a full custody chain with quantities, involved parties, and document hashes.
- Keep document evidence off-ledger in the MVP; bind hashes, content identifiers, credential identifiers, or signed JSON references to custody events.
- Make selective party visibility and one atomic custody transfer central to the Canton demo.
- Avoid broad observer/signatory modeling because it could over-disclose private supply-chain data and weaken the Canton privacy story.
- Add double-spend prevention as a core problem: certified sustainable quantities and proof references should be represented as ledger state that cannot be reused to substantiate multiple downstream commodities.
- Make the canonical demo flow: lot creation, truck transport, silo storage, railway transport, origin port, ship, and destination port.
